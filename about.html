About Me

I'm Nathaniel, a Biomedical Engineering student at Boston University. I'm driven by my curiosity for the teleology and ontology of the natural world and a belief that truth comes from seeing the whole. I'm currently studying engineering with interests in biomedical designs for both neuro and nanotechnology. Outside of academics, I work on personal technical builds, write fiction and analyses, and explore creative projects that connect the abstract to the physical.

  • Systems thinking — I look at the bigger picture, find connections, experiment, iterate, and refine complex ideas into simple, functional solutions.

My Approach

I start every project with research — understanding the landscape, language, and purpose before anything else. There’s a lot I don’t know yet, and learning the fundamentals gives my exploration direction. Uncertainty still unnerves me, but I treat it as the starting point of inquiry; turning the unknown into something clearer is part of the work, even when some questions remain open.
I rely on structured learning because it gives me a foundation I can bend later. Once I understand the rules, I can experiment, warp them, or build something entirely new. My pace shifts with the problem: I move quickly when I recognize patterns, and slow down when depth matters. When things break, I cycle through frustration, reflection, and rebuilding — a process that’s uncomfortable but consistently transformative.
My workflow adapts to the medium. Coding pushes me to iterate, test, and revise, while math or art allows for more linear focus. Still, nothing ever feels truly finished. I revisit projects long after they’re “done,” always finding new angles or possibilities.
